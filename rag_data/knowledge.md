\# Indian Campus Placement Knowledge Base



\## DSA Preparation

Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA) is the most important topic for product-based companies like Google, Microsoft, Amazon. The Striver SDE Sheet is the most popular curated list with 191 problems covering arrays, linked lists, trees, graphs, dynamic programming, and greedy. LeetCode is the standard platform — aim for 200-300 solved problems before interviews. Focus 60% on Mediums, 25% on Easy, 15% on Hard. Topics that appear in 70%+ of interviews: Arrays/Strings, Hashing, Trees (BFS/DFS), Recursion/Backtracking, Dynamic Programming, Graphs.



\## Aptitude Preparation

Aptitude is the biggest filter for service companies (TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Cognizant). Topics: Quantitative (Time-Speed-Distance, Percentages, Profit-Loss, Permutations \& Combinations, Probability), Logical Reasoning (Series, Coding-Decoding, Blood Relations, Syllogisms), Verbal (Reading Comprehension, Sentence Correction, Para Jumbles). Recommended resources: IndiaBix, R.S. Aggarwal Quantitative Aptitude, PrepInsta. Daily practice: 30-50 questions for 4-6 weeks. Aim for 80%+ accuracy.



\## Resume Tips

A strong resume for tech roles fits on 1 page, uses action verbs (Built, Architected, Optimized, Reduced, Led), and includes quantification (numbers, %, performance metrics). Sections in order: Contact Info → Summary (optional) → Education → Skills → Projects → Experience/Internships → Certifications → Achievements. Use ATS-friendly fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman) at 10-11pt. Avoid graphics, columns, and tables that confuse parsers. Save as PDF. Tailor your skills section to the JD.



\## System Design

System design is asked in Tier 1 product companies for SDE roles. Junior candidates face Low-Level Design (LLD) — design a parking lot, splitwise, library system, etc. Focus on OOP principles, design patterns (Singleton, Factory, Observer, Strategy), and SOLID. For senior roles, High-Level Design (HLD) covers scalability — load balancers, caching, sharding, microservices, CAP theorem. Books: "Designing Data-Intensive Applications" by Martin Kleppmann, "System Design Interview" by Alex Xu. YouTube: Gaurav Sen, Tech Dummies.



\## Behavioral Interview (HR Round)

HR rounds test communication, cultural fit, and motivation. Use the STAR framework: Situation, Task, Action, Result. Prepare 8-10 stories covering: leadership, conflict resolution, failure, teamwork, going beyond expectations, handling deadlines. Common questions: "Tell me about yourself", "Why this company?", "Strengths and weaknesses", "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?", "Why should we hire you?". For Amazon, prepare for 16 Leadership Principles deeply. Speak slowly, smile, make eye contact.



\## Top Product Companies (Tier 1)

Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Adobe, Atlassian, Salesforce, Uber, Goldman Sachs. CTC range: ₹18-50 LPA. Hiring process: Online Assessment (OA) → 3-5 technical rounds → HR/Bar Raiser. Required: CGPA 7.5+, 0 active backlogs, strong DSA (300+ LeetCode), at least 1 quality internship. Preparation timeline: 6-12 months. Companies test ability to think under pressure, communicate clearly while coding, and handle follow-up questions.



\## Mid-Tier Product / Unicorns (Tier 2)

Flipkart, Swiggy, Zomato, Paytm, Razorpay, Cred, Zerodha, BYJU'S. CTC range: ₹10-32 LPA. Hiring process: OA → 2-3 technical → HR. Required: CGPA 7.0+, ≤1 backlog, decent DSA (150+ LeetCode), 1+ internship preferred. Often test domain-specific scenarios (e.g., delivery routing for Swiggy, payment flow for Razorpay). Strong portfolio of 2-3 production-quality projects helps a lot.



\## IT Service Companies (Tier 3)

TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Cognizant, Accenture, Capgemini, HCL. CTC range: ₹3.5-11.5 LPA. Hiring process: Aptitude + Coding test (TCS NQT, InfyTQ, NLTH) → Technical interview → HR. Required: CGPA 6.0+, basic coding knowledge, decent communication. Aim for higher-tier roles: TCS Prime (₹9 LPA), Infosys Power Programmer (₹8 LPA), Cognizant GenC Elevate (₹7.5 LPA), Accenture Advanced ASE (₹11.5 LPA). These pay 2-3x the regular role.



\## Internship Strategy

Internships are critical, especially for Tier 1/2 product companies. Best path: pre-final year summer internship at a product company → likely PPO (Pre-Placement Offer). Apply via: company career portals (Google STEP, Microsoft Engage, Goldman Sachs Engineering Campus Hiring), LinkedIn, Internshala, AngelList. Open-source contributions (GSoC, MLH Fellowship, LFX Mentorship) carry significant weight. Aim to start applying 8-10 months in advance for summer internships.



\## Project Portfolio

Quality > Quantity. 2-3 well-built projects beat 8 superficial ones. For each project, you should be able to: explain the problem you solved, justify tech choices (why React over Angular?), discuss scaling challenges, walk through the architecture diagram, and describe one hard bug you debugged. Host on GitHub with detailed READMEs (problem, demo GIF, tech stack, setup, learnings). Deploy live on Vercel/Netlify/Render. Recommended project types: full-stack web app, ML model with API, system tool/CLI, mobile app.



\## Mock Interview Practice

Practice mock interviews 1-2x per week starting 2-3 months before placements. Free platforms: Pramp, Interviewing.io, peer-to-peer with friends. Paid: InterviewBit, Tryexponent. Record yourself and watch back — most students underestimate their tics ("uhh", "like", excessive "basically"). Always think out loud. When stuck, narrate what you've tried, what's blocking you, and what you'd try next. Interviewers value problem-solving process more than the right answer.



\## Communication Skills

Spoken English is critical for Tier 3 service companies (HR rounds heavily filter on this) and helps in Tier 1/2 (technical communication). Daily practice: read aloud 15 min, watch English content (movies/podcasts), have weekly conversations with peers in English. Focus on clarity, pace (not too fast), and confidence. Toastmasters clubs are excellent. For technical communication, practice explaining a concept (e.g., "What is recursion?") in simple terms — to a non-technical person.



\## Backlog Strategy

Active backlogs are a hard filter at most companies. Top product companies (Google, Microsoft) require 0 backlogs. Mid-tier (Flipkart, Swiggy) accept ≤1. Service companies (TCS, Infosys) accept 1, sometimes 2 with re-exam pass. Strategy: clear all backlogs by 6th semester. If you have multiple, prioritize core CS subjects first (DSA, OS, DBMS, OOP, CN) as these come up in interviews. Re-attempts allowed in most universities — use them.



\## Interview Day Tips

Sleep 7+ hours the night before. Eat a moderate meal — not too heavy. Reach 30 min early. Carry: 2 hardcopy resumes, ID proof, pen, notepad, water. Dress: formal (shirt + trousers, blazer optional). Phone on silent. Be confident but humble. If you don't know something, say "I don't know, but I'd approach it by..." instead of bluffing. Always have 2-3 thoughtful questions ready for "Do you have any questions for us?" — never say no.
